Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

The pillars of Bangladesh 2.0 innovation include digital transformation, sustainable development, innovative entrepreneurship, smart infrastructure,, sustainability, and inclusivity.

Sustainability

Bangladesh 2.0 sustainability emphasizes renewable energy, green technology, waste management, climate resilience, biodiversity, sustainable agriculture, and eco-friendly industrialization.

Infrastructure

The pillars of Bangladesh 2.0 infrastructure include smart cities, efficient transportation, renewable energy systems, modern utilities, and sustainable urban planning for balanced growth and innovation.

Empowerment

Empowerment involves providing individuals or communities with education, resources, rights, and opportunities to enhance self-reliance, decision-making, and social inclusion.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur and economist, founded Grameen Bank, pioneering microfinance to alleviate poverty. Awarded the 2006 Nobel Peace Prize, he empowered millions, especially women, through small loans. Yunus’s innovative approach transformed development economics, emphasizing social business and financial inclusion for sustainable poverty reduction worldwide.
